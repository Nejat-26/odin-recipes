Description

Atkilt Wot is a hearty, comforting vegan dish made with cabbage, carrots, and potatoes slowly stewed with aromatic spices. It's often served during fasting days when animal products are avoided. The vegetables are gently cooked with turmeric, garlic, and onions in oil, creating a mildly spiced and fragrant stew.

This dish is simple, nutritious, and a staple in Ethiopian households. It's typically served with injera, but it's also delicious with rice or flatbread.

Ingredients

  • ½ head of green cabbage, chopped
  • 2 medium carrots, sliced
  • 2 medium potatoes, peeled and cubed
  • 1 medium onion, sliced thin
  • 3 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon ground turmeric
  • ½ teaspoon salt (or to taste)
  • ¼ teaspoon black pepper
  • ¼ cup water
  1. Heat vegetable oil in a large pot over medium heat. Add onions and sauté until soft and translucent, about 5 minutes.
  2. Add garlic and turmeric, stir for 1 minute until fragrant.
  3. Add the chopped cabbage, carrots, and potatoes. Stir well to coat with the spices.
  4. Pour in water, cover, and simmer on low heat for 25–30 minutes, stirring occasionally, until vegetables are tender.
  5. Season with salt and black pepper to taste.
  6. Serve warm with injera, rice, or flatbread.
